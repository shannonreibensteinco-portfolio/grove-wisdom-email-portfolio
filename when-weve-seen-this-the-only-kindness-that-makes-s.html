Hi Shannon,

"You've grown a human and kept them alive for a whole year. This deserves to be celebrated!" My friend's handwritten not struck such a chord,it was so timely, I kept the card. I still have it.

I still remember the time a friend popped by my office with one piece of my favorite chocolate and a handful of rose petals she'd pulled off of a Knock-out rosebush outside (they bloom in cycles all year here and deadhead themselves🤩).

She unceremoniously flung the petals like confetti, set the chocolate on my desk, smiled and left. Completely made my day.

It's this...

"Then it is only kindness that makes sense anymore,
only kindness that ties your shoes
and sends you out into the day to gaze at bread,
only kindness that raises its head
from the crowd of the world to say
It is I you have been looking for,
and then goes with you everywhere
like a shadow or a friend."

You can read the whole poem, Kindness by Naomi Shihab Nye here.

When another human takes a minute to let us know, they see us, know us, and like making our life a little kinder, we are transformed. 

It's why I added this little bit of whimsy into the shop this past summer. Go ahead, let someone see your friendship and hold it in their hands. (Here's a free little something extra to help).

It's also the most powerful moment of transformation when this kind of active kindness shows up and sits with us when we suffer. (Naomi Shihab Nye's full poem speaks to this).

One of my favorite regenerative gardeners, Stephanie Rose,f is doing a whole email series with tips on how to be kind to yourself during the holidays by staying connected to nature. Foraging for your own holiday wreaths with neighborhood friends, wrapping gifts in jute twine and clippings from your garden, and bunches of other nature-guided ways of being a little extra kind—gets you grounded and nourished by nature then sharing it with people close to you.

When we cook a meal for someone we love, drop a spontaneous present bomb, make a special cocktail/mocktail/special cup of tea, take time for ourselves as we need it not as we're told to—we are taking a radical stand for active kindness. We are offering our creativity and presence in whatever moment we choose. We are offering the possibility of transformation. 

My shop owner friend, Therese of Geneveive and Claire takes the time every evening to pick three things that brought her joy. It's worth a follow, her joy is expansive, you start to see your own life differently.

I hope your days are met with unexpected kindness and you have the energy and space to reflect on your joy and expand your active kindness.

With love for the planet,🍂🌼 

Shannon and the Grove Wisdom team.
