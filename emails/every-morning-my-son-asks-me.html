Storytime starts here:📖
When my boys were both little, and people would ask what their favorite color was, they tended to get confused. To be fair, adults do tend to ask pretty weird questions. And it was usually about matching things to their bedroom. It was fine. But the moment they came home and said, pink is a girl color, well, I over-reacted.  Big time.
 
I launched into a history lesson of Victorian Era men wearing pink, I said favorite colors don't have to be just one thing. I even said--utterly exasperated--I don't think I have a favorite color anymore, all colors are for everyone. They averted eye-contact and waited. (Were they in trouble) Then I breathed a little and calmed down and apologized later for being a little crazy about color. 
 
But the most surprising thing happened after that.
 
I had taken my son to art gallery shows most of his life. It was part of my job back then. I showed him crazy instagram posts of new media exhibits. I talked about how abstract artist mash-up colors that shouldn't work to help us feel things that are hard to describe.
 
Side note: My husband used to joke that he's sweet because his momma bathed him in sugar. (Did I mention we live in Texas. And I grew up in the yankee state of OHIO.)
 
My kids had a momma 🛀 who bathed them in art.
 
And then they surprised me. They got interested and started asking me what my favorite color was, every morning. "Momma, what's your favorite color today?" And it didn't bother them that most of the time I said, "I don't know." Today I think it's nectarine orange or stormcloud grey. It turned into a game. They never felt like they needed to deviate from their soon established favorites but they did start ranking them. Orange first, then Red, then blue (because brother loves blue). 
 
And they decided there was one color they did not like. Pink for one and yellow for the other. But it's okay, because all colors are for all people and they could accept people prefer different things. Clearly my favorite color today is this Greenish Aqua Marine!
