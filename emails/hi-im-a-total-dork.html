Hi Shannon,

As the year winds down, my dork side is coming out in full force. I'm reflective and vulnerable to big-vision stories of nature repairing itself. Like this one👇 in the Pacific Northwest.

Undamming the River

To me, reflection jumps into motion with poetry. Here's a short one from Mary Oliver.

I'm not the River

I'm not the river

that powerful presence.

And I'm not the black oak tree

which is patience personified.

And I'm not redbird

who is a brief life heartily enjoyed.

Nor am I mud nor rock nor sand

which is holding everything together.

No, I am none of these meaningful things, not yet. 

I heard someone say recently, I don't know how the algorithm could understand me because my taste is so eclectic. Casablanca followed by Real Housewives. Barbie and Openheimer. 

I dork out about mushrooms. It's part of my own "confuse the algorithm" agenda. They've been here on the planet as long as there has been a planet, they live in all ecosystems and they could heal or kill you depending on which species. As a family, they seem to be a gift, saving us from excessive detritus all the time.

And I'm almost ready to accept that I am not yet many many things that I want to become. And I'm often quieted by how gracefully and quickly nature can heal and regenerate. And I embrace that I will always be a dork, too excited about weird things.

Give yourself the gift of kindness. Let your "confuse the algorithm" ambitions guide your internet searches and your off the grid adventures. Dork out about something and fall into a learning rabbit hole that makes you weirdly excited. Embrace your contradictions. And then please, show me how.
