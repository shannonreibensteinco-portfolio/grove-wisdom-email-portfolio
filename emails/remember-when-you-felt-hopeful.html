Hi Shannon,

Isn't Hope peculiar? Watching nature rebound with just a little bit of effort is my happy place. 

It's a deep breath of hope air. Ya that sounds weird. But when the world events hit as hard as they have been lately, regular deep breaths are critical for just managing the anxiety and fear.

And Hope is peculiar. Unexpected. It looks forward with a real possibility of positive change. Like 👇

When forests come back to life in half the time predicted, like this story, “In the past few years we have seen around 2.5 million acres restored.”

That's deep breaths of hope. 🌼

  "It's so revelatory to see that nature can come roaring back from almost complete devastation. That's the story that we've enjoyed telling around the world, and I think has given a lot of folks hope   around the world, nature can make a comeback."

                 -Sean B. Carroll, Author of Endless Forms Most Beautiful, professor of molecular biology and genetics at University of Wisconsin-Madison. 

Sharing is caring.

🐞@homegrownnationalpark [This non-profit is helping support everyday people across the country extend the impact of native habitats preserved by our National Parks into everyone's backyards😍]

🐞@nativehabitatproject [A forester and conservationist out of Alabama, Kyle is restoring a native ecosystem in real time, with grant money to turn the space into an outdoor classroom for local elementary schools.]
